John Ireland: The Man of Programmatic Orchestral Works

Background:

John Nicholson Ireland, born on the 13th of August, 1879,died on 12th of June, 1962. He was an English composer and teacher of music. The majority of his output consists of piano miniatures and of songs with piano. His best-known works include the short instrumental or orchestral work "The Holy Boy", a setting of the poem "Sea-Fever" by John Masefield, a formerly much-played Piano Concerto, the hymn tune Love Unknown and the choral motet "Greater Love Hath No Man".
